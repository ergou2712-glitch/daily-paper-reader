## 摘要
理解可靠信息如何在相互连接的群体中产生，是社会科学、网络理论和数据分析领域的一项挑战。许多现有方法将真相视为外部参考或单个陈述的属性，而非网络本身的全局一致性特征。我们提出了一种基于网络的方法，其中真相源于相互作用个体组成的多层系统（multiplex system）中的全局关系相干性（global relational coherence）。节点是具有内部状态的个体，而边则捕捉不同类型的相互作用，包括声明的关系、观察到的行为、影响力的不对称性以及信息交换。我们评估了节点状态与合作或对抗性相互作用的对齐程度，并纳入了强制力、变异性以及个体言行不一的情况。在包含一千个节点的合成网络上进行的模拟表明，最小全局不一致性并不等同于多数意见或简单的平均值。对不一致性贡献最大的节点在不同相互作用层之间产生了冲突约束，从而定义了一个可测量的扭曲场（distortion field）。例如，在选举期间的在线社交媒体中，少数在不同群体间传播不一致或操纵性信息的账号可能会破坏整体的相干性，即使大多数用户看起来达成了一致。这些结果表明，该方法在评估关系相干性、识别不可约的不一致性以及分析集体状态的约束方面具有潜在应用价值。因此，真相可以被视为最大关系相干性的状态，而非简单的共识或单个陈述的正确性。

## Abstract
Understanding how reliable information emerges in interconnected populations is a challenge in social science, network theory and data analysis. Many existing approaches model treat truth as an external reference or a property of individual statements, rather than a global consistency feature of the network itself. We introduce a network-based approach in which truth arises from global relational coherence in a multiplex system of interacting individuals. Nodes are individuals with internal states, while edges capture different types of interactions, including declared relations, observed behavior, influence asymmetries and information exchange. We evaluate how well node states align with cooperative or antagonistic interactions, incorporating coercion, variability and mismatches between what individuals say and what they do. Simulations on synthetic networks of one thousand nodes show that the minimum global inconsistency does not coincide with majority opinion or simple averaging. Nodes contributing most to inconsistency create conflicting constraints across interaction layers, defining a measurable distortion field. For example, in online social media during an election, a small number of accounts spreading inconsistent or manipulative information across groups can disrupt overall coherence, even when most users appear to agree. These results suggest possible applications in assessing relational coherence, identifying irreducible inconsistencies and analyzing constraints on collective states. Therefore, truth can be seen as the state of maximal relational coherence, rather than simple agreement or correctness of individual statements.
